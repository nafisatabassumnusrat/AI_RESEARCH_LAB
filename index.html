Who We Are

About The Lab

A multidisciplinary research environment where innovation meets rigor.

Background

Founded in 2018 under the Computing & Information Sciences (CIS) Department, our lab was established to address real-world challenges through applied and theoretical AI research. Over six years, we have grown from a small group of five researchers to a dynamic team spanning multiple disciplines.

We are housed in the Technology Innovation Center on campus, equipped with high-performance GPU clusters, robotics platforms, and dedicated data science workstations.

Vision

We envision a future where AI acts as a partner to humanity — augmenting human capabilities, accelerating scientific discovery, and solving problems that were previously considered unsolvable.

Our long-term vision is to be a globally recognized center of AI excellence, producing research that is not only technically cutting-edge but also ethically grounded and socially responsible.

Goals

  • Publish high-impact AI research in top-tier venues.
  • Train the next generation of AI scientists and engineers.
  • Bridge the gap between academic research and industry application.
  • Foster international research collaborations.
  • Develop open-source tools and datasets for the community.

Our Contribution

The lab has contributed to society through practical AI deployments in healthcare diagnostics, smart city systems, and environmental monitoring. Our NLP tools have been adopted by three regional hospitals to assist with medical record analysis.

We run annual AI workshops and hackathons, opening our facilities to undergraduate students and providing mentored research opportunities to over 60 students per year.

Publications

Peer-reviewed work published in top-tier AI venues — NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR, and beyond.

2024 Machine Learning

Adaptive Reinforcement Learning for Real-Time Clinical Decision Support Systems

We propose an adaptive RL framework that learns patient-specific treatment pathways, achieving a 31% improvement in decision accuracy over static baselines across three hospital datasets.

NeurIPS 2024
Md. Sarwar Hossain · Md. Mehedi Hassan · Nusrat Begum
View Paper
2024 Computer Vision

MedScan: Anomaly Detection in Radiology Images via Self-Supervised Contrastive Learning

MedScan introduces a contrastive self-supervised pipeline for X-ray and MRI anomaly detection that reaches 94.3% sensitivity without requiring manually labeled pathology data.

CVPR 2024
Md. Mehedi Hassan · Shahriar Ahmed Israfil
View Paper
2023 NLP

LinguaBridge: Cross-Lingual Transfer for Low-Resource South Asian Languages

LinguaBridge leverages multilingual pre-trained transformers combined with community-curated parallel corpora to achieve state-of-the-art BLEU scores on Bengali English and Urdu English translation tasks.

EMNLP 2023
Md. Asif Mahmud Ridoy · Layla Mansoor
